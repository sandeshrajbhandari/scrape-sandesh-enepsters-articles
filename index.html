Brief Intro to SOMES

SOMES is an independent student body that comprises of Mechanical Engineering students throughout the country. It is responsible for promoting and organizing training workshops and educational fairs, that enhances the curriculum and student participation in this field. Lately, the club has organized training workshops on 3D-printing, Solidworks 3D modelling training and MATLAB workshops. Moreover, the student body has organized 7 successful exhibitions in the past with high student participation.

MECHTRIX 8.0

The main event is accompanied by two major side events viz. Pulchowk Math-Physics Olympiad and ISCEN (“the 1st International Symposium: Control, Energy, Nano-Engineering”). These events will be held before the start of the actual exhibition and are intended for different audiences.

The Math-Physics Olympiad is an academic competition to test the academic prowess of high-school science students in Nepal.

The ISCEN symposium is a seminar on Control Systems, Renewable energy and nano-engineering. Scholars and Engineering Students from different colleges will be attending this event, where several national and international industry experts will share their knowledge on the subject matter.

Mechtrix 8.0, itself, consists of various mini-events that will be held during the 3-day fair.

Major attractions of the fair are:

BE Design Competition

The BE Design Competition is the core of this exhibition and has been conducted since the start of the fair. It invites students of bachelor’s engineering to participate and design machines of many uses and functions.

+2 Mechanical Design Competition

It is similar to the BE design competition but invites high school students to compete with each other by fabricating their ideas into reality. The requirements for entry is much less strict than BE design competition and the projects can have more flexibility.

Drone Competition

In this competition, drone pilots and makers compete to finish a race track. It is conducted with active collaboration with RAN(Robotics Association of Nepal) and invites participants of all nature.

Robo-ball

This event hosts robots that are designed to play football. The rules of the game are same as football and robots compete with each other to get to the top.

Camera

There is no easy way to tell this, but the dual cameras do not live up to the expectations. One, because delivering a promising dual camera setup requires optimized software and data to get the ‘Portrait’ pictures right, and two because the camera hardware isn’t just good enough.

Like many other dual camera phones, the ‘Portrait’ mode is more like a gimmick than a good feature. The phone does take some good portrait photos by blurring the background, focusing on the person. But you are likely to get blurs on the edges.

The feature, however, should get better with time, like the iPhone 7 Plus’s portrait mode did if, and it is a big if Gionee adheres to the standards set by the Apple.

Performance

To our surprise, the phone performed better than expected. The slightly less favored MediaTek processors runs main apps like Facebook and YouTube without any lags. Gaming on the big screen is also entertaining and doesn’t heat up the phone until a long time. Try playing heavy 3D graphics game and then you might suffer. Other than that, ordinary multi-tasking and watching movies is more than good enough.

What else?

What completes the phone is the little features that add up. The software is Android 7.0 Nougat with the Amigo UI on top. There are easy optimizations for users like quick settings access by swiping from the lower screen and redesigned icons ‘inspired’ by certain non-Android pioneers.

The fingerprint scanner at the home button is fast and responsive and while the phone has a big screen draining power rapidly, the phone offers a capable 4550mAh battery. Well done Gionee.

The phone’s sensor provides satisfactory images and when focused properly under suitable lighting conditions also clicks the picture with good details. However, the autofocus is not so good due to lack of some viable technology such as PDAF. The camera suffers in low-lighting conditions with dark pictures with image noise and reduced sharpness.
